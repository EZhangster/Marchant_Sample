What is this course exactly ?

This course is an intensive 5 day lecture and lab course, designed to give biology teachers basic experience in recombinant DNA techniques. The course is run as as partnership between three charitable organizations - Villiers Park Charitable Trust, the Wellcome Trust and the Goldsmiths Company. The course assumes no prior experience at all, and will consist of lab practicals held at the Sanger Centre (Human Genome Campus, Hinxton, Cambridge), lectures (by the course coordinators and visiting scientists) and seminars hosted at Villiers Park (Foxton, Cambridge). The provisional schedule for the summer course can be found here.

What will be covered by the lecture seminars and speakers ?

Aside from explaining theoretical basis of the practicals, we will discuss recent research developments in genetic methods and molecular pathology (e.g. AIDS, cancer, atherosclerosis). There will also be talks from researchers at the Sanger Centre, as well as seminars on genetic policy and healthcare issues from researchers at the University of Cambridge.

How do I apply for this course ?

Application is coordinated through the Goldsmiths' Company. A preliminary advertisement for participants was published in the Spring Times Educational Supplement. Please send all registration correspondence to the Goldsmiths' Company. For application inquiries at other times please contact Villiers Park.

How much does the course cost ?

The course is FREE OF CHARGE to all twenty participants. Attendees are expected to cover their own travel expenses. Conditions for reimbursement of supplementary travel expenses can be discussed by contacting the management of Villiers Park.

The pastoral, laboratory and administartive expenses for this course have been met by generous contributions made by the Villiers Park Charitable Trust, the Goldsmiths' Company and the Wellcome Trust, BioRad and Promega.

What facilities are available at Villiers Park ?

Villiers Park is a custom built educational establishment in the village of Foxton, Cambridgeshire that has hosted student reading parties and teachers courses for many years.

Accommodation will primarily be in single en suite rooms. All meals will be provided by the catering staff on site, and provision for vegetarian options is available if requested prior to each meal.

Villiers Park has bicycles available for touring the surrounding area, a tennis court (bring racquets if you wish to play), and a nearby village shop and pub. The Foxton railway station has hourly (at worst) services to Cambridge and is on the London main line ( non-direct service)

Please address all questions concerning pastoral aspects of the course to the director of Villiers Park, Deborah Richardson.

Do I need to bring anything?

All lab equipment will be provided. However, if you do have a lab coat, please bring it. Stationary will not be supplied, so please bring plenty of your own.

For the final course dinner, semi-formal dress will be required. Please bring a suit/dress suit/dress for this event.
