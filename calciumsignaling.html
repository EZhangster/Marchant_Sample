Functional Architecture of Ca2+ signaling

The business of Ca2+ signaling is one of information delivery, and it’s hard to think of any cellular process that is not regulated by Ca2+. Ca2+ signals resulting from activation of intracellular Ca2+ channels drive this regulation. Dysfunction of these same Ca2+ channels is involved in neurodegenerative disease, as well as cardiac abnormalities and cancer. To appreciate how cell functions are controlled by Ca2+ signals, and how pathological cues subvert their function, we must understand how both the distribution and the properties (the ‘functional architecture’) of intracellular Ca2+ channels controls the genesis of Ca2+ signals. Currently, we are interested in the architecture of Ca2+ signaling between organelles, with projects studying how this communication is established and is utilized under particular cellular conditions.
