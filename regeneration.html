Ca2+ & Regeneration

Ca2+ signals direct cell fate, whether through direct transcriptional regulation or indirectly by regulating the excitable cell niche. We are interested in the role of Ca2+ in directing stem cell differentiation, and our current work examines regeneration of planarian flatworms as a (supposedly) simple paradigm for understand how Ca2+ channel activity can bias regeneration and tissue differentiation toward particular outcomes. We are examining whether ‘pharmacological engineering’ – using drugs to engage particular Ca2+ fluxes in vivo – can be used to yield specific cell types during regeneration. Check out our drug-induced two-headed planarians as a proof of concept and the basis for our chemical genetic screens in the laboratory. This is pure, simple, enjoyable basic science: with potential impact for studying human diseases from neurodegenerative conditions to infectious disease.
